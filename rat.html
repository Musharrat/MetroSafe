A Look at Rats in the NYC Subway

Underground Life

New York City is famous for its vast subway system—and infamous for its underground rodent population. From darting across platforms to appearing in viral videos, rats have become a symbol of life below the streets. But what's really going on in the tunnels? How many rats are down there? And why are they so hard to control?

Quick Stats

Estimated number of rats in NYC:

2–3 million

Stations with most reported rat sightings:

Union Square, Times Square, Grand Central

Common rat species:

Norway Rat (Rattus norvegicus)

Rat control budget (MTA + city efforts):

Over $30 million/year
